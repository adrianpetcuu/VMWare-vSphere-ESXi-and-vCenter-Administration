## SATA and SAS

SATA and SAS are storage
interface technologies used
to connect disks to servers
and computers.

---

## SATA (Serial ATA)

### Characteristics
- Designed for desktops and laptops
- Lower cost
- Slower compared to SAS
- Supports one device per channel

### Typical Use
- Personal computers
- NAS devices
- Non-critical storage

### Speed
- Up to 6 Gb/s (SATA III)

---

## SAS (Serial Attached SCSI)

### Characteristics
- Designed for enterprise servers
- Higher performance and reliability
- Supports multiple devices
- Full-duplex communication

### Typical Use
- Data centers
- Enterprise servers
- Mission-critical systems

### Speed
- 12 Gb/s or higher

---

## SATA vs SAS Comparison

| Feature | SATA | SAS |
|------|------|------|
| Performance | Lower | Higher |
| Reliability | Standard | Enterprise-grade |
| Cost | Cheap | Expensive |
| Use Case | Desktop / NAS | Servers / Datacenters |

---

## Compatibility
- SAS controllers can use SATA disks
- SATA controllers cannot use SAS disks

---

## Summary
SATA is cost-effective
for general use, while
SAS offers higher performance
and reliability for enterprise
server environments.
